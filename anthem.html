Anthem

Video Game / September 2017-August 2018 / Internship

The Project

Anthem is an online multiplayer action video game developed by Canadian game developer BioWare.

My main responsibility over my year-long internship was to board out cutscenes throughout the game. I also completed miscellaneous 2D art as requested from my department.

Tools

  • Photoshop
  • After Effects
  • Frostbite

Roles

  • Storyboarding
  • Digital Art

Storyboarding

The storyboarding process was a collaborative effort that involved several teams and steps.

First, I created an initial draft of boards based on a script from the writing team. I then pitched the boards to a team of individuals from narrative, animation, art, and audio departments. The feedback and suggestions I recieved from the team would be then incorporated into revising the boards. Each scene I worked on went through multiple rounds of revision depending on the complexity of the scene.

The boards I created were used as a visual tool to coordinate with other departments such as writing and level design. Completed boards were then passed on to other departments to carry out the next steps.

With each new scene and iteration of boards I was able to continuously work on my storyboarding skills: use of cinematography principles, clear composition, draftsmanship, pacing, linking shots together, and pitching and presenting.

Animatics

For some scenes I was required to put my boards together into a animatics. These rough animatics were a way to gauge timing and pacing of the scene, and were also another format used to present to other departments.

Reflection

The learning experience was a very valuable part of my internship. I learned a lot about the different teams and work that goes into triple A game development. A sense of collaboration runs through everything, and I gained valuable experience working with many departments of various sizes and disciplines.
